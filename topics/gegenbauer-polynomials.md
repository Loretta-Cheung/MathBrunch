# Gegenbauer Polynomials

## Role in the roadmap

Gegenbauer polynomials are the orthogonal polynomial basis naturally suited to spherical problems.

## Current goal

The goal is reading fluency, not full mastery.

A useful first understanding:

- spherical symmetry suggests using special orthogonal polynomials;
- coefficients in this basis carry positivity information;
- LP bounds depend on coefficient signs;
- spherical designs can be detected through vanishing moments.

## Questions

- Why are Gegenbauer polynomials the right basis for the sphere?
- What is positive definiteness in this setting?
- How do moments connect to spherical designs?
- How much of this machinery is needed for small examples?
