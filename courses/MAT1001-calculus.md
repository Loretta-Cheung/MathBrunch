# MAT1001 — Calculus

## Role in the roadmap

Calculus provides the analytic language behind potentials, energy functions, approximation, and optimization.

## Core concepts

- limits and continuity
- differentiation
- integration
- Taylor approximation
- sequences and series
- one-variable optimization

## Research-facing translation

| Course concept | Research-facing concept |
|---|---|
| derivative | local change, monotonicity, convexity intuition |
| Taylor expansion | approximation by polynomial certificates |
| optimization | energy minimization and extremal configurations |
| sequences and limits | asymptotic thinking |
| integration | averaging and continuous measures |

## Study target

By the end of the semester, calculus should not feel like procedural computation only. It should become a language for approximation, constraints, and energy.

## Questions to revisit

- How does Taylor approximation connect to polynomial certificates?
- How does one-variable optimization prepare intuition for energy minimization?
- What kind of calculus is actually needed for spherical code problems?
